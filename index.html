    About Me

    I am currently exploring data science, mathematical modeling, and machine learning at Princeton University. I am looking for opportunities to apply these skills to make impacts in areas that I am passionate about: climate change, investing, and soccer.

    My parents immigrated to the United States from Sweden and decided to stay so my little sister and I could get the best education possible. I grew up in Sarasota, Florida and was very fortunate to have the opportunity to attend Pine View School. I grew a passion for topology and its applications to how we currently understand our universe. I published research on how mathematics can be applied to solving problems in the emerging electric trucking industry and held a symposium at my high school about how mathematics can be applied to careers.

    Passions

    Applied Mathematics

    In March 2020, I worked in an amazing team of four high school seniors to compete in the 2020 MathWorks Math Modeling Challenge. We ended up placing first out of 760 teams nationwide and were fortunate to have the opportunity to publish our work in SIAM Undergraduate Research Online (SIURO). I look forward to continuing to apply mathematics to solve problems that I am passionate about.

    Soccer

    Playing soccer competitively for over 15 years has helped me build effective communication skills and leadership ability. This past year, I was given the opportunity to captain my high school soccer team, and I look forward to continuing to play soccer competitively at Princeton when we arrive to campus.

    Reading

    This year, my New Year's Resolution was to read at least a book per week this year. I managed to read 53 fiction and nonfiction books in 2020, and I look forward to continuing this challenge and aiming for 70 books in 2021. Some of my favorite books are Thinking Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman and Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind by Yuval Noah Harari.
