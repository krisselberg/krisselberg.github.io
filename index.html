About

Me in 10 Seconds

Reader, content creator, Princeton University sophomore, programmer and soccer enthusiast.

First-gen Florida native. Bilingual in Swedish and English.

What I'm Doing Now

I am focused on devoting my weekdays to learning and testing deepfake detection methods with the Qatar Computing Research Institute.

On the weekends and in my free time, I create content on Tiktok and YouTube and post weekly blog posts.

I am living in Florida and spending time with my friends and family before I go back to Princeton University in September for my sophomore year of college.

I am reading books that cover interesting topics like Stoicism, content creation, the universe, and more. Please reach out to me with book recommendations or anything your heart desires!

Past Projects

MathWorks Math Modeling Challenge 2020 Grand Champion

In March 2020, I worked in a team of five to solve problems related to the electric trucking industry, including calculating optimal placements of charging stations on certain routes, predicting the population of electric trucks 5, 10, and 20 years from now, and determining which routes are most important for developing charging stations first. We ended up placing first in the nation out of 760 teams and winning $20,000 in scholarship money. Our paper can be seen here in SIAM Undergraduate Research Online and a video of our presentation can be found here.
